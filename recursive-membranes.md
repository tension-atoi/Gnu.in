# Recursive Membranes

### An algorithmic philosophy in honor of GNU.IN OS

---

**Recursive Membranes** is a generative aesthetic born from a single gesture repeated without end: take a region of space, and divide it. The plane begins whole — one unbroken field — and then a seam appears, splitting it into two. Each half remembers the cut that made it and answers with a cut of its own, perpendicular, balanced, alive. From this one law — *bisect the largest container along its longer axis* — an entire desktop of windows blooms, never the same arrangement twice, yet always legible, always composed. The movement worships the moment a continuous surface becomes a population of surfaces, each one a **membrane**: a taut, gradient-skinned cell that knows where it ends and its neighbor begins.

The computational heart is binary space partitioning carried out with restraint. A meticulously crafted recursion walks the plane, and at every node it weighs a seeded coin: split, or settle. Splits favor the golden mean but are perturbed by controlled noise, so the resulting lattice escapes the deadness of a perfect grid while never collapsing into chaos. Stopping is governed by area and depth — large regions almost always divide, small ones almost always rest — so the eye reads a natural hierarchy of scale, the way a real workspace holds one broad canvas beside a column of slim panes. This is not random rectangles. This is order arrived at through painstaking calibration, the product of deep computational expertise where every ratio, every threshold, every gap was tuned across countless iterations until the tessellation simply *felt right*.

Between the membranes lives the **gap** — negative space made structural. The gap is not empty; it is the breath of the composition, the dark mortar that lets each cell glow as its own object. Around every membrane runs a border drawn as an angled gradient, a thin ribbon of light that slides from warm to cool along the cell's diagonal. One cell among them all is **focused** — chosen by the seed, lifted above the rest by a brighter skin, a halo of soft blur, and a border whose gradient slowly rotates, the only thing in the frame that moves. Everything else holds perfectly still around it. The composition is mostly silent so that this single living element can sing.

Color is treated as material, not decoration. Membranes are filled with low-saturation, glassy gradients — slate, teal, dim violet — that read as frosted panels catching the light of an unseen wallpaper beneath. The accents are reserved for the borders and the focused cell, where saturated warmth meets cool to mark the boundary of attention. The palette must always feel curated by a master colorist: harmonious, deliberate, restrained. Garish RGB has no place here. The beauty is in the relationships — warm against cool, dense against open, bright focus against quiet field — every value chosen to serve the whole.

Each run of the algorithm is a complete, reproducible artifact. A seed is a coordinate in an infinite gallery: feed it the same number and the same desktop reassembles, pixel for pixel; advance the seed and the membranes rearrange into a new but kindred order. This is the Art Blocks covenant — determinism as authorship. The viewer does not paint; the viewer *discovers*, walking the seed space to find the arrangement that resonates, knowing the algorithm could have made it and only the seed revealed it. The work is the system, and the system was built like fine instrumentation — exact, resilient, the master-level implementation of someone at the absolute top of the generative-art craft.

To express this philosophy is to render the soul of a compositor that thinks in space: a runtime that takes the raw plane of a screen and, through one recursive law applied with total discipline, composes it into a living, breathing arrangement of membranes — gapped, gradient-edged, rounded, and quietly aware of which of them holds the light.
