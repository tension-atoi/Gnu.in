## Caption (copy/paste)

> don't mind me. i think i accidentally built an operating system.
>
> it started in march. i wasn't building anything, i was just trying caelestia, someone else's gorgeous hyprland setup, poking at it to make it feel a little more like mine. then i put an ai agent in my terminal and a whole world cracked open. suddenly i could build the little tools i'd only ever wished for. so i did. one, then another, then another.
>
> one thing led to another. every tool i made, i wanted to rework the layer beneath it, questioning my approach, tearing it down, building it back cleaner, mostly in rust, on nights and weekends.
>
> about the ai: i build it with cloud agents, no secret there. but the whole system is operable by a local one too, ollama or vllm, on my own machine. it suggests, i decide, nothing changes without my yes.
>
> and then one day i looked up. a bar, a dock, a launcher, settings, a lock screen, a keyboard, a wallpaper engine. twenty-one little pieces, all running, all mine. that's when it hit me: i'd basically built an operating system. i'd call it that, if i believed in such a thing. it's still very much an experiment, held together with curiosity and late nights, but it's mine and it's real.
>
> started in march as a hobby. somewhere along the way it turned into a whole computer. i'm thinking about giving it away one day, free and open, once i sort the boring legal bits. no promises, no launch.
>
> anyway. back to breaking it.
